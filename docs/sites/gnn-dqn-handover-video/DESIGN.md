# Visual Identity: Clean Technical Defense

## Style Prompt

Use a light, precise defense-presentation style inspired by Swiss Pulse: grid-locked layouts, crisp technical diagrams, calm motion, and professor-friendly clarity. The video should feel like an animated thesis defense slide deck, not a marketing reel. Use cellular-network visuals: maps, graph nodes, UE movement traces, signal beams, batch tensors, replay buffers, and bounded SON parameter knobs.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#F7FAFC` soft technical white
- Ink: `#102033` deep blue-black
- Muted text: `#52657A`
- Telecom cyan: `#0EA5C6` for graph/message-passing accents
- Signal green: `#16A34A` for safe/valid connectivity and SON stability
- Map amber: `#F2B84B` for geography and deployment path highlights
- Risk red: `#D64545` for ping-pong/outage warnings
- Panel stroke: `#D5E0EA`

## Typography

- Primary: Inter, Helvetica Neue, Arial, sans-serif.
- Headline weight: 760-850, compact and precise.
- Body/labels: 400-560, readable from projector distance.
- Numbers: large, tabular-feeling, used as evidence anchors.

## Motion Rules

- Entrances are precise: small y movement, short fades, line draws, graph node pops.
- Transitions are clean geometric wipes with a cyan/green scan line, never glitchy.
- Background motion is subtle: slow map-grid drift, low-opacity signal pulses, moving packet dots.
- Use 0.35-0.75 second entrance timings with `power3.out` or `expo.out`.
- Keep every scene readable for a defense room: no frantic text, no dense paragraphs.

## What NOT To Do

- Do not use dark cyberpunk neon or purple-blue gradients.
- Do not make it look like a product ad or social-media hype reel.
- Do not claim raw `gnn_dqn` is the final deployment result.
- Do not call RSRQ proxy real PRB utilization.
- Do not overclaim universal throughput dominance over A3.
